1. Project Overview

Customer shopping behavior analysis focuses on parsing the patterns, habits, and pathways of consumer purchases. Businesses analyze these interactions to discover buying triggers, track preference lifecycles, and align operational resources. By tracking key patterns such as spend thresholds, purchase timing, and product categories, companies transition from standard sales execution to predictive, customer-centric planning.

This case study features the engineering of an enterprise-grade Power BI dashboard translating transactional retail data into clean visual insights. Designed for cross-functional utilization, the platform delivers strategic dashboards for executive management, visual tracking assets for marketing coordinators, and category breakdowns for sales managers.

The core objective of the project is to build a reliable data model that cleans, models, and exposes transaction statistics. This enables managers to optimize shelf placements, reduce inventory write-offs, run focused promo campaigns, and track customer lifetime values.

2. Business Challenges & Context

Modern retailers capture vast amounts of consumer interaction logs but struggle to translate raw databases into structured corporate strategies. The business challenges addressed by this dashboard include:

  • Customer Retention Deterioration: Inability to trace customer churn rates or segment repeat buyers vs. one-time visitors.
  • Blind Purchasing Profiles: Marketing teams launching general promotions rather than targeted offers aligned with demographic preferences.
  • Seasonal Supply Misalignment: Severe inventory stockouts and excess storage costs due to unmonitored seasonal sales trends.
  • Muted Product Recommendations: Poor understanding of item affinity clusters, resulting in low cross-selling values.
  • ROI Disconnections: Launching discounts without tracking their contribution to net profit margin expansion.

5. Data Cleaning & Preprocessing

A multi-step ETL pipeline was designed to ensure data quality and integrity before ingestion:

  • Deduplication: Removed duplicate transaction records across compound tables using unique combinations of Customer ID, Transaction Timestamp, and SKU code.
  • Handling Missing Data: Imputed missing product review values with category averages. Excluded records missing critical values like Purchase Date or Purchase Amount.
  • Data Type Standardization: Converted Date fields to standard ISO format (`YYYY-MM-DD`). Cleaned transactional string inputs and cast numeric columns to Decimal types.
  • Range Validation: Cleaned datasets to ensure ages fell within a valid 18–100 range and quantities were strictly positive, eliminating negative records from return anomalies.
  • Calculated Column Creation: Engineered helper fields (e.g., `Net Revenue` after discount offsets, `Age Group` ranges, and `Season Code` values).

6. Power Query ETL Steps

Advanced data transformations implemented in Power Query to structure raw transactional files:

  • Unpivoting Tables: Restructured multi-column survey fields into key-value pairs for standardized feedback processing.
  • Conditional Column Engine: Created helper columns like `Discount Status` (Yes/No based on threshold limits) and defined customized `Age Groups` (18-25, 26-35, 36-50, 51+).
  • Date Dimension Generation: Generated a dynamic Calendar dimension table from the transaction table's date boundaries, detailing Year, Quarter, Month, and Week numbers.
  • Merge Queries: Merged product category and master SKU tables to denormalize the dataset, reducing lookup overhead in the data model.

7. Data Modeling (Star Schema)

To optimize performance and query speeds, the database tables were modeled into a Star Schema with clearly separated Fact and Dimension tables.

Relationships are structured as **1-to-Many (1:*)** uni-directional filters flowing from the Dimension tables down to the central Fact table, minimizing calculation paths and eliminating circular filters.

8. Enterprise DAX Calculations

Key DAX formulas engineered for dynamic filter contexts and time-intelligence queries:

Total Sales (Gross Revenue)

Total Sales = SUM('Fact_Transactions'[Purchase_Amount])

Repeat Customer Count

Repeat Customers = CALCULATE( DISTINCTCOUNT('Fact_Transactions'[Customer_ID]), FILTER( VALUES('Fact_Transactions'[Customer_ID]), CALCULATE(COUNTROWS('Fact_Transactions')) > 1 ) )

Customer Retention Rate %

Customer Retention Rate = VAR CurrentPeriodCustomers = DISTINCTCOUNT('Fact_Transactions'[Customer_ID]) VAR PriorPeriodCustomers = CALCULATE( DISTINCTCOUNT('Fact_Transactions'[Customer_ID]), SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR('Dim_Calendar'[Date]) ) VAR RetainedCustomers = INTERSECT( VALUES('Fact_Transactions'[Customer_ID]), CALCULATETABLE( VALUES('Fact_Transactions'[Customer_ID]), SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR('Dim_Calendar'[Date]) ) ) RETURN DIVIDE(COUNTROWS(RetainedCustomers), PriorPeriodCustomers, 0)

12. Data-Driven Business Insights

Key business insights uncovered after analyzing the dataset:

  • Top Customer Segment: Subscribed members aged 26–35 drive over **38%** of total transaction volume.
  • Top Product Category: Clothing and electronics accounts for **52%** of gross sales revenue.
  • Peak Sales Months: Q4 holiday sales (Nov-Dec) generate a **45% increase** in transaction volumes.
  • Payment Choice: Digital wallets and credit cards represent **72%** of total transactions.
  • Top Profit Region: Urban locations represent **68%** of net transaction values.
  • Age Group Patterns: Customers aged 50+ purchase higher-margin items but transaction frequencies are **30% lower** than youth segments.
  • Loyalty Retention: Customers with Gold-tier memberships spend **2.4x more** on average than Silver-tier members.
  • Discount Sensitivity: Orders containing 10%–15% discounts saw a **22% volume increase**, while higher discount tiers reduced net profit margins.
  • AOV Milestones: Subscribed members maintain a **$94 AOV**, compared to **$68** for guest checkouts.
  • Rating Correlations: Average customer review scores dropped during peak holiday shipping cycles, suggesting shipping capacity limitations.
  • Return Anomalies: Apparel returns peaked in January, indicating a need for size guide optimizations.
  • Preferred Purchase Time: Over **60%** of online checkout transactions occur between 6:00 PM and 10:00 PM.
  • Gift Card Efficacy: Promotional gift card campaigns increased repeat purchase rates by **14%** within a 30-day window.
  • First-time Buyer Churn: Guest checkout users have an **80% bounce rate** without making a second purchase within 90 days.
  • Subscription Growth: Loyalty programs saw a **15% year-over-year expansion** in active registrations.
  • Cart Abandonment Rates: High shipping costs during checkout led to a **65% cart abandonment rate** for orders below $50.
